Addictivity – Privacy Policy

Last updated: March 2026

This Privacy Policy describes how Addictivity (“we”, “us”, or “our”) collects, uses, and protects your information when you use the Addictivity mobile and web applications (the “App”).

We are committed to protecting your privacy and handling your personal data in compliance with applicable laws, including the EU General Data Protection Regulation (“GDPR”) and relevant U.S. privacy laws (such as the California Consumer Privacy Act, “CCPA”, as amended).

1. Data Controller and Contact

The data controller for your personal information is the operator of the Addictivity App.

If you have any questions or requests regarding this Privacy Policy or our data practices, you may contact us at:

Email: Management.addictivity@gmail.com

2. Information We Collect

2.1 Information You Provide Directly

  • Account information. When you sign in with Google (via Firebase Authentication), we receive your Google account email address and display name.
  • Profile and settings. Within the App, you may choose a display name, select themes, and configure other preferences.
  • Support communications. If you contact us via email or through in‑app support, we collect the information you provide (such as your email address and the content of your message).

2.2 Information Generated Through Your Use of the App

  • Tasks and skills. We store tasks you create, their due dates and recurrence, associated skills, and whether tasks are completed.
  • Progress data. We store time spent per task and skill, streaks, levels, XP, completion history, achievements and milestones, and other usage metrics used to power the gamified productivity features.
  • Economy and shop data. We store your in‑app “gems” balance, lifetime gems, gems spent, unlocked items (e.g., park decorations and companions), active boosters, and related shop interactions.
  • Device and usage data (app analytics). We may collect limited technical information about how you use the App, such as:
    • Basic device information (device model, operating system version)
    • App version and performance/diagnostic events (e.g. errors, crashes)
    • In‑app navigation (which screens are used and how often)
    This is used solely to maintain and improve the App and is not used for third‑party advertising or cross‑app tracking.

2.3 Information from Third Parties

  • Google (Firebase Authentication). When you sign in with Google, Google provides us with your email address and display name so we can create and manage your account. We do not receive your Google password.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect for the following purposes:

  • To provide and operate the App. Including creating your account, storing tasks and skills, tracking time, calculating levels and streaks, and enabling in‑app economy and shop features.
  • To sync your data across devices. So you can access the same tasks, skills, and progress wherever you sign in with your account.
  • To personalize your experience. For example, showing your current streaks, achievements, and titles, and tailoring in‑app content to your skills and progress.
  • To maintain and improve the App. Including troubleshooting, performance monitoring, and feature development based on aggregated or anonymized usage patterns.
  • To protect our rights and prevent abuse. Including detecting and preventing fraud, abuse, or misuse of the App.
  • To communicate with you. For example, responding to your support requests or important service‑related notices.

4. Legal Bases for Processing (EEA/UK Users)

If you are in the European Economic Area or the United Kingdom, we process your personal data on the following legal bases:

  • Contract (Art. 6(1)(b) GDPR). To provide the App and its core features in accordance with the Terms of Use (e.g. account, tasks, skills, progress, in‑app economy).
  • Legitimate interests (Art. 6(1)(f) GDPR). To maintain and improve the App, prevent abuse, and protect our legal rights, provided these interests are not overridden by your rights and freedoms.
  • Consent (Art. 6(1)(a) GDPR). Where required by law (for example, for certain optional analytics or communications), we rely on your consent. You may withdraw consent at any time where applicable.

5. How We Share Your Information

We do not sell your personal information.

We may share your information in the following limited circumstances:

  • Service providers. We use trusted third‑party providers to host and operate the App, including:
    • Google Firebase. For authentication, data storage, and related backend services.
    • Cloud and infrastructure providers. For servers, databases, and monitoring.
    These providers process data on our behalf and under appropriate contractual safeguards.
  • Legal obligations. We may disclose information if required by law, court order, or governmental authority, or if we believe that such disclosure is reasonably necessary to:
    • Comply with a legal obligation;
    • Protect the rights, property, or safety of us, our users, or others;
    • Detect or prevent fraud or security issues.
  • Business transfers. In connection with a merger, acquisition, or sale of all or a portion of our assets, your information may be transferred as part of that transaction, subject to this Privacy Policy or an equivalent protection.

5.1 No Third‑Party Advertising or Cross‑App Tracking

At this time, the App does not use third‑party advertising networks, ad identifiers, or data brokers for targeted advertising or cross‑app tracking. If we introduce advertising in the future, we will update this Privacy Policy and, where required, request your consent in accordance with applicable laws (including Apple’s App Tracking Transparency requirements).

6. International Transfers

Our services may be hosted in countries outside of your own (for example, on servers operated by Google Cloud/Firebase). Where required by law, we take appropriate measures to ensure that international transfers are protected by suitable safeguards, such as standard contractual clauses or equivalent mechanisms.

7. Data Retention

We retain your personal data for as long as your account is active or as needed to provide you with the App and fulfill the purposes described in this policy.

In particular:

  • Account and profile data are retained while your account is active.
  • Task, skill, and progress data are retained while you maintain an account to enable long‑term tracking and progression.
  • We may retain certain information for longer where required by law, to resolve disputes, or to enforce our agreements.

If you request deletion of your account, we will delete or irreversibly anonymize your personal data, subject to any legal obligations that require us to retain certain information.

8. Your Rights

8.1 Rights for EEA/UK Users (GDPR)

If you are in the EEA or UK, you have the following rights with respect to your personal data:

  • Right of access: To obtain confirmation whether we process your data and a copy of that data.
  • Right to rectification: To request correction of inaccurate or incomplete data.
  • Right to erasure: To request deletion of your data in certain circumstances.
  • Right to restriction: To request that we restrict processing in certain cases.
  • Right to data portability: To receive your data in a structured, commonly used, and machine‑readable format, and to transmit it to another controller where applicable.
  • Right to object: To object to processing based on our legitimate interests.
  • Right to withdraw consent: Where we rely on consent, you may withdraw it at any time.
  • Right to lodge a complaint: With your local data protection authority if you believe our processing violates applicable law.

To exercise any of these rights, please contact us at Management.addictivity@gmail.com.

8.2 Rights for California Residents (CCPA/CPRA)

If you are a California resident, you may have the following rights under the CCPA/CPRA:

  • Right to know/access: To request information about the categories and specific pieces of personal information we have collected about you.
  • Right to deletion: To request that we delete personal information we have collected from you, subject to certain exceptions.
  • Right to correction: To request that we correct inaccurate personal information about you.
  • Right to non‑discrimination: We will not discriminate against you for exercising your CCPA rights.

We do not “sell” personal information as defined by the CCPA/CPRA, nor do we share it for cross‑context behavioral advertising at this time.

To exercise your rights, please contact us at Management.addictivity@gmail.com.
